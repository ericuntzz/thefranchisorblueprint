# High Point precedent — Approved Suppliers

> Source: HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 11 F&B Procurement & Supply Chain, Section 4.1 Required Equipment, Section 12.1 Core Menu)

## What a strong Approved Suppliers chapter looks like

A category-by-category supplier governance document — typically 2–4 pages — that defines which products must be sourced from a designated supplier (Tier 1), which must be sourced from an approved list (Tier 2), and where the franchisee has discretion. The chapter is the operational counterpart to FDD Item 8: it translates legal restrictions into a daily ordering playbook, with par levels, ordering cadences, and a non-compliance escalation path.

## Structure observed

- Approved Supplier Program overview — naming the Franchise Portal as the source of truth and stating that the list is updated regularly
- Category table with supplier requirement and notes per category, e.g.:
  - Espresso & coffee beans: required (proprietary roast — single source)
  - Dairy: approved regional suppliers chosen by market
  - Alternative milks: approved national brands only
  - Syrups & sauces: required proprietary
  - Pastries / food items: approved local or national, must meet quality spec
  - Cold brew concentrate: required or in-house recipe
  - Packaging (cups, lids, sleeves): required branded
  - Cleaning supplies: approved food-safe products
- Ordering cadence and par levels by product type:
  - Coffee & proprietary products (weekly / 7–10 days on hand)
  - Perishables (2–3x weekly, FIFO)
  - Dry goods & supplies (monthly, 30-day par)
- Inventory management requirements: monthly physical counts, delivery verification log, FIFO labeling, waste log, variance threshold for investigation
- Supplier non-compliance protocol: quarantine, document with photos, escalate within 24 hours, await written guidance before disposing
- Material-breach language: "Use of unapproved suppliers for any Tier 1 (required) category is a material breach of the Franchise Agreement"

## Depth indicators

- Categories are tiered explicitly — proprietary required vs. approved list vs. franchisee choice.
- Each category specifies *who* maintains the approved list (corporate vs. regional).
- Par levels are stated in days-of-supply, not dollar amounts — survives volume variation across units.
- Variance threshold for inventory investigation is a named percentage (e.g. >2% of COGS triggers a written explanation).
- Non-compliance protocol is a multi-step process with timeframes, not a vague "report it."
- Connects to the Franchise Agreement — names use of unapproved suppliers as a material breach.
- Equipment vendors handled separately and treated as a distinct approved-vendor list (Section 4.1) — equipment is not the same as F&B procurement.

## Tone & voice

Operational, prescriptive, FDD-aware. Uses language like "required," "approved," "permitted" with precision — the words have legal weight. Names categories before naming brands. Avoids implying the franchisor earns rebates from approved suppliers without disclosing it (Item 8 territory).

## What to avoid

- Vague "use approved suppliers" guidance with no named categories or tiers.
- Single-source requirements that aren't justified by brand consistency or proprietary specs (read as rent-seeking).
- Par-level guidance in dollar amounts (breaks at scale).
- Supplier non-compliance procedures that don't define escalation timeframes.
- Listing specific vendor names in the chapter itself — those belong in the Portal where they can be updated; the chapter governs the *categories* and *rules*.
