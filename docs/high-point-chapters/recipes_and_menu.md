# High Point precedent — Product & Service Specs

> Source: HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 12 Menu Standards & Recipe Compliance), HP_Barista_Certification_Program_v2_1.docx (Modules 6, 9, 13, 14 — drink menus, flavor system, cold brew, blended drinks)

## What a strong Product & Service Specs chapter looks like

A precise, recipe-card chapter — typically 20–40 pages, often the second-longest after Operations — that defines exactly what the franchisee is licensed to sell, how each item is prepared to spec, and what tolerance is allowed in the recipe. The strongest version pairs *what's on the menu* (Core Menu definition) with *how to make it* (recipe spec sheets) and *when corporate will change it* (LTO and pricing-change processes). The reader should be able to make every drink to brand standard from this chapter alone.

## Structure observed

- Core Menu definition: required categories with examples (espresso drinks / brewed coffee / tea & non-coffee / blended beverages / pastries & food / retail), notes on minimum SKU counts per category, recipe-spec compliance requirement
- Recipe-compliance requirements: espresso parameters as numeric ranges, milk ratios by beverage size, syrup-pump standardization by cup size, temperature ranges, presentation specs (cup style / lid type / sleeve placement)
- Drink menu by category, each with size table:
  - Hot drinks shot count by cup size
  - Drink-by-drink prep table (espresso style / cup size / espresso shots / milk texture / notes) for ~10 core hot drinks
  - Iced drinks with cold brew quantity, ice level, fill instructions per cup size
  - Specialty / seasonal recipes (decaf handling, apple cider, steamers, misto, tea hot/iced)
- Flavor system reference:
  - Available flavors listed in a 4-column grid (regular thin, regular thick, sugar-free)
  - Flavor pump count by cup size, distinguishing thin vs. thick syrup, hot vs. iced/blended
  - Flavor combination rules ("when combining more than one thin flavor, subtract one pump of each")
  - Drink-specific exceptions (named drinks where the rule changes)
- Cold brew SOP: making procedure (grind, soak, settle, pull) with time thresholds (8 hours minimum soak, 30-minute post-massage settle), in-drink quantities per cup size, par-level guidance ("4–6 brews in fridge minimum")
- Blended drinks cheat sheet: scoop count and milk volume by cup size, smoothie measurements, notes on extra-shot adjustments to keep total liquid oz consistent
- LTO (Limited-Time Offer) process: nationally announced LTOs are mandatory; franchisee LTOs require 30-day advance approval; LTO recipes and pricing fixed by corporate
- Menu pricing rules: suggested retail set by corporate, pricing variance bands (+15% / -10% before approval), pricing changes go through Franchise Portal and are pushed by corporate

## Depth indicators

- Espresso parameters stated as numeric ranges with three independent variables (dose / yield / time) — an actual spec, not "make it good."
- Recipe spec sheets are pushed via Portal with a 14-day training-update obligation on the franchisee.
- Pump counts in a single table covering 5 cup sizes (12 / 16 / 20 / 24 / 32 oz) and three syrup categories (thin iced/blended, thin hot, thick).
- Flavor exception table is a closed list of named drinks where the standard rule does not apply — caramel macchiato, blended chai, vanilla bean, hot chocolate, italian cream sodas, apple pie cider.
- Cold brew has *both* a making procedure and a pulling procedure with explicit "do not disturb the bag" and "30-minute settle after massage" rules — process knowledge, not just recipe knowledge.
- Iced beverage rules include an absolute prohibition: "Never pour shots straight over ice" and "Iced americanos should never feel warm to the touch" — concrete sensory failure modes.
- Blended-drink math has an explicit rule for keeping total liquid oz consistent when adding extra shots ("use 2 oz cold brew but only 6 oz of milk") — preserves drink quality at the recipe level.
- Smoothie note: "add a couple extra oz of liquid over the recipe — they never quite make enough" — encodes practical kitchen wisdom.
- LTO compliance is mandatory for nationally announced offers, with launch-date and ordering obligations.
- Pricing variance bands are explicit (+15% upper / -10% lower) before approval is required.

## Tone & voice

Recipe-card register. Compact, imperative, tolerance-aware. Heavy use of tables. Inline tip callouts ("Add flavors BEFORE anything else — easy to forget once you start building the drink") that read like trainer voice, not corporate voice. Treats the bar as an engineered system: dose, yield, ratio, temperature.

## What to avoid

- "Make it to taste" instructions — destroys consistency across the system.
- Recipes without cup-size variants — every drink lives in 4–5 sizes, each with different ratios.
- Open-ended pricing freedom — leads to system-wide menu inflation/deflation.
- Skipping the LTO compliance language — without it, franchisees opt out of seasonal pushes and dilute brand campaigns.
- Recipe specs that ignore the *workflow* (sequencing milk before shots, drying baskets, immediate use) — the chapter should encode timing, not just ingredients.
- Naming individual products by full proprietary recipe in chapter prose — those belong in versioned recipe spec sheets pushed via the Portal so they can be updated without re-issuing the chapter.
