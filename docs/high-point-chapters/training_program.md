# High Point precedent — Training & Certification

> Source: HP_Barista_Certification_Program_v2_1.docx (full document — 14 modules + practice test + sign-off sheet), HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 3.4 Training Program Overview)

## What a strong Training & Certification chapter looks like

A leveled, gated training curriculum — typically 40–80 pages — that takes a new hire from zero knowledge to certified independent operator across measurable stages. The strongest version structures certification as a *ladder* (Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3 → Trainer/GM), with each level gated by a written exam at a named pass rate, a practical assessment, and a recorded sign-off. It pairs the curriculum (modules) with the assessment (exams + practical demos) and the credentialing (signed certification sheet retained in the employee file).

## Structure observed

- Program overview with certification levels in a table: Level / Timeline / Modules Covered / Requirements (written exam %, practical assessment, latte art / speed drill milestones)
- Alignment to an external standard (e.g. SCA — Specialty Coffee Association) named explicitly
- Modules organized by craft area, ordered by skill progression:
  - Module 1: Domain fundamentals (Level 1)
  - Module 2: Core technical skill foundation (Level 1)
  - Module 3: Advanced calibration / dialing in (Level 2)
  - Module 4: Step-by-step preparation SOP (Level 1)
  - Module 5: Process science (milk chemistry / steaming) (Level 2)
  - Module 6: Menu & recipes (Level 1)
  - Module 7: Mastery techniques (latte art) (Level 3)
  - Module 8: Bar setup & ancillary processes (Level 1)
  - Module 9: Modifier system (flavor pump rules) (Level 1)
  - Module 10: Workflow & service hospitality (Level 1/2)
  - Module 11: Food safety & hygiene (Level 1)
  - Module 12: Equipment care & troubleshooting (Level 2)
  - Module 13: Specialty processes (cold brew) (Level 2)
  - Module 14: Blended drinks cheat sheet (Level 1)
- Each module contains: SOPs, troubleshooting tables (problem / cause / fix), tip callouts, common mistakes
- Module 15: Practice Test — multiple sections (Hot / Iced / Blended / Misc) with closed-reference assessment policy ("cheat sheets allowed during practice; closed reference on the official test")
- Certification Sign-Off Sheet: per level, lists modules completed, written-exam score, practical demos passed, with trainer signature and date — retained in employee file and uploaded to franchisor's Portal within 7 days

## Depth indicators

- Three certification levels with rising pass rates (80% → 85% → 90%) — escalating standard.
- Each level has *both* written and practical assessments — not just one.
- Modules tagged by which level they belong to — allows partial-progress hiring (you can hire someone Level 1 certified to start).
- Specific skill milestones at each level: Level 2 dial-in within 5 minutes; Level 2 latte art = heart; Level 3 latte art = tulip/rosetta + train-the-trainer demo + rush simulation.
- Troubleshooting matrices per module (e.g. machine leaking around portafilter / no steam pressure / grinder jammed) — diagnostic, not just procedural.
- Tip callouts encode trainer voice ("Don't let your shots expire — once pulled, espresso shots are only good for 15 seconds") — wisdom, not just rules.
- Practice test mirrors the certification structure — same sections, same recipe coverage — so trainees can self-assess.
- Sign-off sheet lists modules required at each level, exam minimum scores, and specific practical pass/fail items with trainer signature.
- Certification records have a 7-day upload SLA to the franchisor's Portal — auditable training data.
- Training timeline (Weeks 1–2 / 3–6 / 7–12) ties cleanly to the Employee Handbook's 30/60/90-day milestones.
- Cross-references to the franchisor's GM Certification track as a separate program (not part of the barista ladder).

## Tone & voice

Trainer voice. Imperative, pattern-rich, mistake-anticipating. Uses "you" addressed to the trainee. Mixes technical precision (numeric ranges, timing, temperatures) with hospitality (the 5-Foot Rule, "education vs. snobbery," recovery protocol). Tip callouts use a distinct visual marker (★ or !) that signals "this is the kind of thing trainers say out loud at the bar."

## What to avoid

- Single-level training programs that conflate "trained" with "certified" — without progression, advancement loses meaning.
- Pass/fail gates on opinion ("trainer signs off when the candidate is ready") — replace with a named score on a written exam plus a named practical demo.
- Training content that lives only in the franchisor's head — every module should have a written SOP that survives turnover at the franchise location.
- Certification records held only at the location — the franchisor's Portal needs the upload to audit system-wide skill levels.
- Mixing "good attitude is enough" hiring language into the training section — that belongs in the Franchisee Profile chapter, not the curriculum.
- Training programs that don't include a troubleshooting matrix — you teach baristas to react when things go wrong, not just when they go right.
