# High Point precedent — Site Selection & Territory

> Source: HP_Site_Selection_BuildOut_Guide.docx (full document — 10-step process, scoring system, lease guidance), HP_Salt_Lake_Site_Broker_Scorecard.docx (rapid evaluation tool), HP_Site_Evaluation_Report.docx (full submission form), HP_Location_BuildOut_Manual_v2.docx (build-out portion of the same workflow), HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 9 Site Selection & Real Estate, Section 10 Build-Out)

## What a strong Site Selection & Territory chapter looks like

A multi-document workflow — typically 30–50 pages across two or three companion files — that turns "find a good spot" into a defensible, scored, gated process. The strongest version separates concerns into three coordinated documents: a *broker scorecard* (1–2 pages, rapid triage), a *site evaluation report / submission package* (10+ pages, full submission), and a *site selection guide + lease negotiation playbook* (the master framework). Each gates franchisor approval, and no lease may be executed without written franchisor approval.

## Structure observed

- Master guide framing: who uses this (franchisees, brokers, contractors, franchisor team), why site selection is mission-critical (30–50% sales variance based on location alone, 10–20 year financial commitment), 10-step process from trade-area definition to lease execution
- Trade-area analysis: radius bands by density (1-mile urban / 3-mile suburban / 5-mile rural / drive-time alternatives), data sources named (Esri, CoStar, Placer.ai, Census, Google Street View)
- Site location type ranking: Hard Corner > Soft Corner > Mid-Block > Freestanding, with automatic point deductions for non-ideal types
- Center type ranking: Freestanding > Neighborhood > Community > Power Center > Mall, with explicit deduction values (-3 / -5 / -5 / -4)
- Proprietary scoring system: 100-point starting score, deductions for negative factors, letter-grade scale (A+ 97–100 / B– 80–82.9 / F below 60), B– as minimum acceptable threshold
- Deduction tables by category: Ingress/Egress (left-in / left-out / traffic light status / one-way penalty), Visibility (trees, outparcels, hill, signage), Parking (internal traffic, landscaping, lighting, field size)
- Final score worksheet with starting points minus categorized deductions
- Companion broker scorecard: weighted 0–5 evaluation across 12 categories (trade area demos / commuter pattern / access / visibility / parking & stacking / drive-thru feasibility / co-tenancy / competition / occupancy economics / utilities / lease flexibility / brand fit), category weights summing to 100
- Go/No-Go gates: 6 critical requirements where any "No" = immediate rejection (drive-thru permitted, safe ingress/egress, visibility from commuter corridor, no restrictive co-tenancy, occupancy costs aligned with revenue model, no fatal zoning issues)
- Broker Q&A list: 10 questions to confirm before LOI (exclusive use clauses, CC&R restrictions, signage rights, drive-thru approval history, electrical capacity & upgrade cost, grease interceptor, patio rights, delivery access, hour restrictions, TI allowance)
- Red flags / immediate disqualifiers list (divided highway with no median cut, hidden building, drive-thru lane <6 cars, head-to-head competitor at same intersection, landlord resistant to drive-thru, massive utility upgrades, NNN/CAM blowing out occupancy ratio)
- Lease negotiation playbook: minimum 10-year term, 2x 5-year options, TI allowance benchmark range per square foot, free rent / build-out period, assignment clause, exclusivity clause, lease red-flag checklist
- Site Evaluation Report submission package contents: completed scorecard, trade-area report, site info (address / rate / landlord), minimum 10 photos, lease/LOI documents, maps
- Build-out manual handles the post-approval phases: pre-lease task list, contractor selection, design approval, construction milestones, FF&E standards, brand imaging, inspections, pre-opening walk-through (see operating_model.md for build-out cross-reference)

## Depth indicators

- Three companion documents at three different fidelities: rapid scorecard / full submission / master guide — the franchisee uses each at a different stage.
- Scoring system uses 100-point starting score with named deductions — every deduction is observable, not subjective.
- Letter-grade thresholds map to recommendations (A– or higher = strongly recommend; B-/C+ = requires justification; below 60 = automatic rejection).
- Minimum acceptable score named (B– / 80 points).
- Go/No-Go gates short-circuit the scoring entirely — six items where any failure rejects the site regardless of score.
- Broker scorecard has explicit category weights (e.g. Access & Ingress/Egress weighted 3, Drive-Thru Feasibility weighted 2, Brand Fit weighted 1) — forces priorities.
- Drive-thru feasibility called out as a separate scoring category — recognizes the operating model.
- Lease negotiation guidance benchmarks rent (PSF/year) by market tier (A / B / C metros), TI allowance ($/sq ft), and free-rent period.
- Lease red flags include unlimited personal guarantee — protects the franchisee.
- Occupancy ratio threshold named (8–12% of projected gross sales is the benchmark, >12% is a red flag).
- Submission package requires minimum 10 photos and specific demographic data — auditable, not anecdotal.
- Site approval is *written*, must be obtained before lease execution, and any lease executed without written approval may be rejected for opening under the brand.
- Cannibalization protection: protected territory radii of at least ~1.5 miles between units (named in market-strategy work).

## Tone & voice

Real-estate-broker register. Numerate, gate-driven, exception-aware. The voice expects pushback ("but the rent is great…") and pre-empts it with hard go/no-go gates and quantitative scoring. Uses urgency about long commitment ("a poor decision here is very expensive to correct later") rather than hype.

## What to avoid

- Subjective site evaluation ("good visibility, decent parking") with no scoring framework.
- Skipping the go/no-go gate layer — without it, a high-scoring site with a fatal zoning flaw still gets recommended.
- Lease negotiation guidance without rent benchmarks — the franchisee has nothing to anchor against.
- Site approval as informal verbal sign-off — must be written, signed, and on file before lease execution.
- Combining site selection and build-out into a single document — they're sequenced workflows with different audiences (broker/landlord vs. architect/GC) and need to live separately even when cross-referenced.
- Letting franchisees execute leases on unapproved sites and then evaluating after — the order matters: scorecard → franchisor approval → LOI → lease.
