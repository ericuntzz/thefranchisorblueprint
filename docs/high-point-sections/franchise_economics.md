# High Point precedent — Royalty, Ad Fund & Fees

> Source: HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 5.1 Royalty & Fee Structure, Section 6.1–6.2 BMF / LSM), HP_Utah_Market_Strategy_Report.docx (Recommended Franchise Economics Guardrails), HPMF_Policy_and_Procedure_Manual_v1_0.docx (collections mechanics)

## What a strong Royalty, Ad Fund & Fees section looks like

A precise, table-driven section — typically 2–3 pages — that names every dollar a franchisee will pay the franchisor, when they pay it, and what they get for it. The reader should be able to plug the numbers straight into a spreadsheet and have nothing left to ask. It pairs the *number* with the *rationale* — why this royalty rate, why this ad-fund split — so the franchisee understands the model rather than just memorizing rates.

## Structure observed

- Single fee table: row per fee, columns for Amount / Cadence / Calculation Basis / Notes
  - Initial franchise fee (one-time, due at signing)
  - Royalty fee (% of Gross Sales, weekly ACH)
  - Brand Marketing Fund / National Ad Fund contribution (% of Gross Sales)
  - Local Store Marketing minimum (% of Gross Sales, spent locally per quarter)
  - Technology fee (flat $ or per-location, monthly)
  - Renewal fee
  - Transfer fee
- Definition of "Gross Sales" — what's included, what's excluded, with an FDD cross-reference
- Collection mechanics: ACH cadence, who initiates, what happens on a returned payment (NSF fee), interest on overdue
- "Good standing" definition and what franchisees lose access to when not in good standing (co-op buys, council eligibility, premium assets)
- Recommended-rate rationale showing how the rates compare to the competitive set (with a benchmark table of 4–6 peer concepts)
- Fee waiver / credit conditions — force majeure, store transfer, robbery — naming the franchisor's discretion
- Note that initial fee is non-refundable; reference to Franchise Agreement governing in conflicts

## Depth indicators

- Royalty + BMF stated as separate line items, not bundled into a single "service fee" number.
- LSM is positioned as a *minimum spend*, not a fee paid to the franchisor — clear distinction.
- Collection cadence is explicit (weekly ACH on a named day, monthly on the 1st, etc.).
- "Gross Sales" definition handles edge cases (gift cards, comp, third-party delivery commissions, sales tax exclusion).
- Late-payment interest and NSF fee are named in dollar / percentage terms.
- Benchmark table positions the concept against named peers and explains *why* the chosen rate sits where it does ("matches Beans & Brews; below the 6% category ceiling").
- Tech fee is broken out separately — not buried inside royalty.

## Tone & voice

Precise, contractual, neutral. Reads like a finance spec. Every number has a unit, a cadence, and a calculation basis. Avoids justification language ("we believe this is fair") — instead lets the comparison table do the work. Cross-references the Franchise Agreement repeatedly and reminds the reader the FA governs.

## What to avoid

- Bundled fee descriptions that hide the BMF inside the royalty.
- Royalty rates with no peer benchmark — leaves the franchisee unable to evaluate fairness.
- Ambiguous "Gross Sales" definitions that invite disputes (sales tax, third-party delivery commissions, gift card redemptions).
- Implying any fee is negotiable when it is not.
- Sales-pitch language about "investing in your success" — this section is about money, not motivation.
