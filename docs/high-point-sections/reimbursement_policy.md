# High Point precedent — Expense Reimbursement Policy

> Source: HPMF_Reimbursement_Policy_v1_0.docx (full document)

## What a strong Reimbursement Policy looks like

A short companion document — typically 6–10 pages — that defines how the franchisor pays back franchisees and council members for marketing-fund-related expenses. Most marketing-fund disputes happen at the reimbursement layer (a franchisee submits a claim, doesn't get paid, escalates), so the section's job is to make every step verifiable and every deadline strict. The policy lives downstream of the Marketing Fund Governance manual and explicitly defers to it on conflicts.

## Structure observed

- Purpose, scope, and deference clause ("In conflict, the master manual governs.")
- Guiding principles: direct payment first, documented or denied, digital by default, franchisee-friendly timing
- Section 1 — Trade & vendor credit reconciliation: what counts as trade, submission cadence (quarterly default / monthly optional), strict quarter-by-quarter deadlines (redemption + submission dates), reimbursement rate ($0.35 per $1.00 face value with cost rationale), submission contents checklist, verification workflow with named SLAs (5-business-day first-pass / 30-day payment), serial-number tracking, dispute process with named appeal window (15 business days)
- Section 2 — Personal & council reimbursements: enumerated eligible expense categories, documentation standards, required co-signature matrix (who can authorize whom), proof-of-payment threshold ($1,000+), submission & payment timeline, list of non-reimbursable categories (alcohol, charitable contributions, fines, upgraded airfare, etc.), travel reimbursement guidelines
- Section 3 — Defaults, audits & recovery: not-in-good-standing consequences, audit rights, recovery of erroneous payments, annual reconciliation as part of independent CPA audit
- Section 4 — Forms & references, payment timeline quick-reference, related documents, amendment history

## Depth indicators

- Trade reimbursement rate is a *specific* number ($0.35/$1.00) with a stated rationale (blended COGS on trade product + admin cost + industry benchmarks).
- Quarterly deadlines named to a specific calendar date per quarter (not "30 days after quarter end") — leaves no interpretation.
- Verification workflow has named SLAs at every stage: 5-day first pass, 30-day payment, 10-day dispute response.
- Co-signature matrix is a 4-row table mapping submitter role to required co-signer.
- Proof-of-payment threshold is set at $1,000 per line item — concrete number, not "significant amounts."
- Eligible expense categories are positively enumerated; everything else is excluded by default ("If the expense is not on the list above, the HPMF will not reimburse it.").
- Non-reimbursable list is specific and behavioral: alcohol, personal meals outside per-diem, charitable contributions, professional association dues, fines/penalties, airfare upgrades.
- Travel guidelines name carrier policy ("standard economy"), advance-booking minimum (30 days), lodging tier ("moderate-tier or franchisor block rate"), and meal handling (actual receipts or per-diem).
- Audit rights include vendor verification — franchisor can call the supplier to confirm services were rendered.
- Default-state consequences are enumerated: any reimbursement is held until standing is restored, or applied against outstanding balances.

## Tone & voice

Procedural, deadline-driven, accounting-aware. Voice assumes the reader will look for loopholes and closes them in advance. Reads like an internal-audit document with consumer-friendly framing on top. Heavy use of tables and explicit numbers; minimal narrative.

## What to avoid

- Vague reimbursement timelines ("processed in a timely manner").
- Trade-credit rates without cost-basis rationale — invites accusations of skimming.
- Open-ended eligible expense lists — should be a closed enumeration with a default-deny.
- Skipping the dispute process — every reimbursement policy needs a documented appeal path.
- Mixing reimbursement rules into the master marketing-fund manual rather than splitting it as a companion document. The split keeps the master cleaner and lets the reimbursement policy be amended independently.
