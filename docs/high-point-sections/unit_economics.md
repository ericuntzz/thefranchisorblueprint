# High Point precedent — Unit Economics & Financial Model

> Source: HP_Utah_Market_Strategy_Report.docx (current AUV estimates, benchmark table), HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Section 5.4 Financial Performance Benchmarks), LMF-Franchise Development Strategy and 12-Month Gantt Chart.docx (Investment Overview template — partially filled)

## What a strong Unit Economics section looks like

A short, sober section — typically 2–4 pages — that lays out the cost structure to *open* a unit, the cost structure to *run* a unit, and the historical performance the franchisor can defensibly point to. The job is to give a prospective franchisee enough to do real math, while staying inside FDD Item 7 / Item 19 guardrails. It is *not* a pitch deck. The strongest version names the legal disclaimer up front and sticks to ranges and benchmarks rather than a single forecasted number.

## Structure observed

- Total investment range (low–high) — single headline number with a clear range
- Initial investment table broken into ~10–14 standardized cost categories: franchise fee, real estate / build-out, equipment & FF&E, signage, opening inventory, technology, professional fees, grand opening marketing, training travel, insurance prepay, working capital reserve, contingency
- Ongoing fee structure: royalty %, brand marketing fund %, local marketing minimum %, technology fee
- Operating-cost benchmark table: COGS %, labor %, prime cost ceiling, occupancy %, average ticket range
- Current AUV / revenue estimate for company-operated units (with explicit "internal estimate / not audited" caveat if Item 19 has not been finalized)
- Comparable-brand benchmark table showing where this concept sits in the category (franchise fee, royalty, AUV)
- Revenue & profitability projections by year (Ramp / Stabilization / Maturity)
- Break-even and ROI timeline indicators
- Heavy legal disclaimer block: "This is not an FDD. Item 19 governs."

## Depth indicators

- Investment range is a *range*, not a single number — and the spread is realistic (typical full range is 50–100% wide).
- Ongoing fees broken out by both percentage AND collection cadence (weekly ACH, monthly, quarterly).
- Benchmarks are stated as windows, not point values: "Prime cost below 65% of gross sales," not "prime cost is 60%."
- Comparable-brand table cites the source (FDD summaries, brand websites) and includes 5–7 direct competitors.
- AUV figures are flagged as audited or unaudited; if unaudited, the section explicitly says these cannot be marketed without Item 19 work.
- Working-capital reserve is sized in months of operating cash (e.g. 3–6 months) rather than a flat dollar figure.
- 15% contingency line is named and sized.
- Year-by-year P&L summary distinguishes ramp vs. stabilized — does not assume Year 1 = mature run rate.

## Tone & voice

Numerate, conservative, lawyer-aware. Reads like it was written by someone who has been through an FDD review. Uses ranges over claims, benchmarks over forecasts. Disclaimer language is direct, not buried. No earnings hype.

## What to avoid

- Single-point AUV claims with no source, range, or sample size.
- Marketing-doc language ("massive ROI," "explosive growth," "passive income").
- Investment ranges that don't include working capital or contingency.
- Skipping the "internal vs. audited" distinction on existing-unit revenue figures.
- Reproducing competitor financials without attribution.
