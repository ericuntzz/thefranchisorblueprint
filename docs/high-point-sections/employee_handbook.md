# High Point precedent — Employee Handbook

> Source: HP_Employee_Handbook_v2.docx (full document), HP_Document_Review_Franchise_Audit.docx (cross-cutting findings on franchise-readiness vs. corporate-only language)

## What a strong Employee Handbook looks like

A standalone team-member-facing document — typically 20–30 pages — that the franchisee distributes to every new hire and that doubles as legal scaffolding (at-will, EEO, anti-harassment) and culture-setting (welcome letter, values, hospitality standard). The strongest version is location-agnostic: every store-specific detail (hours, manager names, payroll system, alarm vendor) is a bracketed placeholder so a single template works across the system. It ends with an acknowledgment of receipt that the employee signs.

## Structure observed

- Welcome letter from leadership (warm, owner-to-team voice)
- Mission / Vision / Core Values block (mirrors the franchisor's brand section)
- Section 1 — Culture & values: who we are, what we expect, the hospitality standard
- Section 2 — First 90 days: introductory period definition, 30/60/90-day milestones, new-hire attendance policy, training path
- Section 3 — Compensation & pay: pay schedule, overtime, recording hours, tips, pay reviews
- Section 4 — Scheduling & attendance: schedule posting cadence, attendance standards, calling out, shift covers, no-call/no-show consequences
- Section 5 — Time off & leave: time-off requests, sick leave, leaves of absence, jury duty / voting
- Section 6 — Benefits & perks: employee drink/food benefit, unemployment, workers' comp, growth & development
- Section 7 — Uniforms & appearance: dress philosophy + concrete standards
- Section 8 — Workplace conduct & rules: expectations, grounds for immediate termination, social media, personal devices
- Section 9 — Safety & health: hazards specific to environment, accident reporting, chemical handling, food safety
- Section 10 — Non-harassment & EEO: definitions, reporting, retaliation prohibition
- Section 11 — Drug-free workplace: prohibited conduct, testing circumstances, support
- Section 12 — Performance & development: review cadence, progressive discipline (4 stages), certification & advancement
- Section 13 — Grievance & open-door policy
- Section 14 — Separation: voluntary resignation, involuntary termination, final paycheck, return of property
- Section 15 — Legal notices & employment-at-will, handbook updates
- Acknowledgment of receipt page (signed, dated, copy retained by employee)

## Depth indicators

- Location-agnostic placeholders throughout: `[Location Name]`, `[Franchisee Business Name]`, `[Payroll System]`, `[Scheduling System]`, `[Security/Alarm System]` — no store-specific names hard-coded.
- Attendance policy defines the rolling window in days (e.g. 120-day rolling), states the math explicitly ("two late arrivals = one unexcused absence"), and names the threshold for action ("more than 3 unexcused in 120 days").
- 30/60/90-day milestones are observable and measurable, not subjective: "complete POS transactions independently," "manage drive-thru window during a morning shift," "pass certification at 80%+."
- Termination grounds enumerated as a closed list — not "any other reason management deems necessary."
- Progressive discipline is a 4-step ladder: verbal → written → final written → separation, with explicit franchisor right to skip steps for severity.
- Drug-testing circumstances enumerated (reasonable suspicion / post-accident / dangerous-equipment operators), with state-law caveat.
- Anti-harassment section names protected categories explicitly and prohibits retaliation against reporters.
- Equal-opportunity statement names the *franchisee* as the employing entity, not the franchisor — preserves the no-joint-employer posture.
- At-will language flagged as subject to state law — explicit reminder for franchisees to consult counsel.
- Explicit "this handbook is not a contract" disclaimer.
- Acknowledgment of receipt page includes employee name, signature, date, manager signature, and a note that the employee retains a copy.

## Tone & voice

Plain language, employee-facing. Warm in the welcome and culture sections; crisp and unambiguous in the policy sections. No jargon. Treats the reader as an adult. Uses "you" and "we" — avoids third-person abstraction ("the team member shall…"). The audit document's biggest call-out: avoid burying new-hire rules inside general rules — give them their own labeled subsection.

## What to avoid

- Hard-coding store-specific details (hours, manager names, payroll vendor, security vendor) — these vary by franchisee.
- Naming the *franchisor* as the employer in EEO/at-will language. The franchisee is the employer; the franchisor is not.
- Vague attendance language ("twice in 120 days constitutes an absence") — make the math explicit.
- Subjective milestones ("works toward improvement," "shows good judgment") that can't be assessed.
- Open-ended termination clauses that swallow the progressive discipline framework.
- Mixing training content (drink recipes, espresso glossary) into the handbook — that belongs in the training program.
- Referencing a different brand by name (the audit found a "wrong brand name" still embedded in a draft policy — full find-and-replace before any distribution).
