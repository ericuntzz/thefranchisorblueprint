# High Point precedent — Marketing Fund Governance

> Source: HPMF_Policy_and_Procedure_Manual_v1_0.docx (full document, all 8 sections)

## What a strong Marketing Fund Governance section looks like

A complete governance manual — typically 15–25 pages, the longest section in the system — covering how the marketing fund is collected, who decides how it's spent, how decisions get made, and how franchisees challenge them. The reader should finish able to answer: "If I don't like how the BMF is being spent, what's my recourse?" This is the section most likely to become the focus of franchisee disputes if written poorly.

## Structure observed

- Section 1 — Fund overview: purpose, benefits, three-tier spending structure (Chainwide / Regional / Local), default allocation ranges, Fund Use Test ("must benefit multiple franchisees")
- Section 2 — Council role & structure: governing body composition, regional definitions, activation thresholds
- Section 3 — Chainwide Advisory Council: initial vs. permanent council composition, eligibility, electronic nomination & election timeline (specific dates), candidate disclosure, voting rules, quorum, confidentiality
- Section 4 — Agency partners: Agency of Record scope, AOR responsibilities, brand asset distribution, AOR selection/replacement process
- Section 5 — Financial responsibility: collections mechanics, good-standing definition, disbursement authorization, signatory requirements, no-deficit-spending rule, reporting cadence (quarterly statements within 45 days, annual independent CPA audit within 120 days), interest income, account credits, excess funds & reserves, vendor marketing dollars
- Section 6 — Advertising policies: approved channels (digital, social, streaming, traditional, OOH, influencer, loyalty/CRM, research), confidentiality of campaign info, communications protocols
- Section 7 — Regional & local activation: market assignment, outlying-store policy, local council formation thresholds (X stores + Y owners), local council composition / term structure / responsibilities, eligible & prohibited uses of local funds, joint market campaigns, invoice & documentation standards, expenditures benefiting one store, reimbursement for market-wide promotions, prize award thresholds, gift disclosure, default & non-compliance
- Section 8 — Definitions, standard forms, transparency / conflict-of-interest policy, amendment history

## Depth indicators

- Default allocation given as *ranges* (e.g. Media 60–70%, Creative 15–25%) rather than fixed percentages — Council has flexibility.
- Activation thresholds named in concrete numbers: "10 stores + 3 distinct owners + 1 qualifying DMA" triggers a Regional Council.
- Election process has a real timeline with named dates and deadlines (nomination opens first business day of June, etc.) — not vague "annually."
- Quorum, voting majorities, and Franchisor override authority all defined separately.
- Spending decisions above defined thresholds (e.g. >15% of annual collections on a single initiative) require both Council majority AND written Franchisor approval.
- Prohibited uses of local funds enumerated (a long, specific list — awnings, business cards, holiday decorations, individual store websites, charitable donations, etc.).
- Invoice documentation standards specify proof-of-performance by channel (tearsheets, broadcast affidavits, screenshots, platform analytics).
- Reimbursement turnaround is committed (30 days from complete submission).
- Conflict-of-interest policy is referenced as a standalone document and summarized.
- Annual independent audit by a CPA is mandatory and posted to the Portal within 120 days.

## Tone & voice

Governance-document register. Procedural, structured, dispute-aware. The voice expects that the section will be quoted in a future disagreement and writes accordingly — every claim has a named rule and a named decision-maker. Uses "shall" and "must" deliberately. Tracks "NEW in v1.0" call-outs to flag what changed from prior policies.

## What to avoid

- Spending category descriptions without ranges or caps.
- "The Council decides" without naming quorum, voting majority, or Franchisor override.
- Fixed allocation percentages that lock the Council into a stale plan.
- Missing the audit/reporting cadence — quarterly statements and an annual independent CPA review are both essential.
- Skipping the prohibited-uses list — its absence guarantees disputes about edge cases.
- Treating local marketing as an afterthought — the local council mechanics are the section's most operationally important content.
