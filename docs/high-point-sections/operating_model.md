# High Point precedent — Daily Operations

> Source: HP_Franchise_Operations_Manual_v3_0.docx (Sections 0–8 + Section 13 — pre-opening roadmap, store SOPs, staffing, equipment, financial reporting, marketing, technology, legal, health/safety/risk)

## What a strong Daily Operations section looks like

The thickest section in the system — typically 30–60 pages — and the centerpiece of the franchise relationship. It defines exactly how a unit runs from the moment the first team member arrives to the moment the last light goes off. The strongest version is sequenced by *time* (pre-opening → opening shift → service hours → closing → reporting cycles) and is dense with checklists, par-level tables, and threshold numbers. It is the working playbook, not a marketing document.

## Structure observed

- Section 0 — Pre-Opening Roadmap: 8 phases from franchise award through post-opening support, each with a timeframe and key milestones
- Section 1 — Brand Standards & Identity: brand story, mission/vision/values, logo usage, brand colors with full color-system specs, typography, brand voice, prohibited brand uses
- Section 2 — Store Operations & SOPs: hours of operation, opening procedures (60-minute and 30-minute checklists), closing procedures (60-minute and at-close checklists), guest experience standards, service timing standards by beverage category, quality control parameters
- Section 3 — Staffing, Training & HR: minimum staffing requirements by shift, roles & responsibilities (GM / Shift Lead / Barista with required certifications), hiring standards, training program overview (Levels 1–3 + GM), performance management (30/60/90 reviews, progressive discipline, documentation)
- Section 4 — Equipment & Facilities: required equipment list, daily / weekly / monthly / annual maintenance schedules, facility standards for customer areas and back-of-house
- Section 5 — Financial & Reporting Requirements: royalty & fee structure, reporting cadences (weekly POS uploads / monthly P&L / quarterly business review / annual audited financials), banking & cash handling, financial performance benchmarks (COGS, labor, prime cost, occupancy, ticket size)
- Section 6 — Marketing & Local Store Marketing: BMF, LSM minimum spend, social media guidelines, grand opening program
- Section 7 — Technology & POS: required tech stack, POS configuration & menu management restrictions, data & reporting, technology support tiers, internet bandwidth minimums
- Section 8 — Legal, Compliance & Obligations: agreement overview, licenses/permits/insurance, food safety & health compliance, audit rights, advisory council, contact directory
- Section 13 — Health, Safety & Risk Management: food safety program (HACCP, temperature logs, allergen management), incident reporting protocol with timeframes by incident type, emergency preparedness plan, workplace safety
- Each section uses tables, numbered checklists, and explicit threshold numbers
- "Note:" callouts throughout flag exceptions, escalations, and the franchisor's reservation of rights

## Depth indicators

- Pre-opening phases mapped to specific timeframes (Day 0, Months 1–3, Months 2–4, etc.) and named milestones (LOI submitted, lease executed, GM Certification complete).
- Hours of operation are stated by day with a 30-day-advance change notification requirement.
- Opening procedure broken into 60-minute and 30-minute pre-open windows, each a numbered checklist of ~6–8 specific tasks.
- Quality-control parameters stated as numeric ranges, not adjectives: espresso dose 18–21g, yield 36–42g, time 25–32s, milk temp 140–150°F (extra hot max 160°F).
- Service-timing standards by beverage category in target seconds/minutes — turn the kitchen into a system, not a guess.
- Staffing minimums stated by shift type (opening / peak / closing / solo not permitted).
- Reporting cadences: weekly POS upload by Monday 11:59 PM for prior week; P&L by 15th of following month; FBR call quarterly; audited financials by March 31.
- Cash-handling thresholds: max $200 in register overnight, voids/refunds over $25 require Shift Lead authorization, register discrepancies over $10 must be investigated within 24 hours.
- Financial benchmarks given as ranges: F&B COGS 28–32%, labor 28–35%, prime cost <65%, occupancy 8–12%, average ticket $8–$14.
- Audit rights enumerated: unannounced inspections, financial review, mystery guest, third-party audits.
- Insurance requirements specify minimum amounts ($2M GL per occurrence / $4M aggregate, $1M umbrella, business interruption equal to 12 months gross revenue) and the franchisor as named additional insured.
- Incident reporting matrix maps incident type → reporting timeframe → required action — a 7-row table covering injuries, food safety, theft, equipment failure, media/social-media crisis.
- Internet requirement: 100 Mbps down / 25 Mbps up minimum, business-grade only, dedicated POS line that "must never be shared with any other device."

## Tone & voice

Operational, prescriptive, table-driven. The voice assumes the reader is the GM running the unit at 5:45 AM with a checklist in hand. Direct verbs ("Disarm," "Turn on," "Pull and date-label"). Threshold numbers everywhere. Reservation-of-rights language is present but never inflated — when something is at the franchisor's discretion, the section says so plainly.

## What to avoid

- Aspirational SOPs ("strive for excellence in every cup") — replace with measurable parameters (dose / yield / time / temp).
- Single-shift staffing as a permitted configuration — solo operation creates safety, theft, and quality risk.
- Reporting cadences without named due dates and named systems for upload.
- Mixing "should" and "must" carelessly. The section must be read as a contract; "must" means contractual obligation, "should" means recommended practice.
- Food-safety sections that don't enumerate timeframes for incident reporting (4 hours / 24 hours / immediately).
- Ambiguous escalation paths — every incident type needs a named recipient and timeframe.
